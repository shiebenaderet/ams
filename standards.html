WA Social Studies Learning Standards • Grades 6–8

See how the same skills stack across middle school—from World History in 6th, to Washington State in 7th, to U.S. History in 8th.

Washington’s 2019 standards are written as a single 6–8 band, but each standard’s components are mapped to a grade and regional focus, so the skill grows across three years on the same throughline. For example, “analyze multiple perspectives” is practiced on world history in 6th grade, Washington State in 7th, and U.S. history in 8th.

The four content strands (Civics, Economics, Geography, History) follow this World → WA → U.S. progression. The Social Studies Skills strand (inquiry, reasoning, deliberation, argument) is a continuous through-line practiced at all three grades.
